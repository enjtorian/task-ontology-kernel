### Contributions

- **TOCA Architecture Design**: Formalized the Task-Oriented Cognitive Architecture (TOCA) — a closed-loop cognitive system enabling tasks to be captured, dispatched, executed, validated, and evolved.
- **Pattern Naming & Taxonomy**: Named the five-layer conceptual stack (POG → TOK → TOCA → POG Task → POG OS), establishing a complete vocabulary for the Task-native paradigm.
- **Documentation & Architecture**: Complete whitepaper authorship, rigorous system design, and implementation guides for both English and Traditional Chinese.

### Background
As a software systems architect with over 20 years of experience, I noticed a fundamental shift while repeatedly using LLMs: prompts are no longer disposable instructions—they are reusable cognitive structures. This insight revealed a deeper truth—**tasks are evolving from mental units of humans into execution primitives of systems**.

**Behind the immense execution power of LLMs, a critical gap became evident:** we granted AI the agency to modify systems, yet lacked a structural unit, like a Git Commit, to define the boundaries of behavior.

TOK is not an invention but a formalized description of a natural evolutionary process. Just as Unix didn't invent the concept of "file" but was the first to formalize it as a system primitive, TOK is the first to formalize "task" as a system primitive natively executable by AI.

The patterns described here were already happening independently across teams; they just needed a name. By making these invisible cognitive structures visible and governed, we accelerate the conversation about Task-native software engineering.
